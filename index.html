UAE CV format — key differences from India
  • 1–2 pages maximum. ATS-clean format. No decorative design, no infographic elements, no coloured sidebars. Dubai recruiters process high volumes — anything that doesn't parse cleanly in ATS gets rejected before a human sees it.
  • Include nationality and visa status. Standard in UAE — not discriminatory. Format: "Indian national — seeking employment visa sponsorship" or "Willing to relocate to Dubai." Employers need this to plan visa logistics.
  • Photo: Not mandatory but common in UAE. If included, use a professional headshot against a plain background. If unsure, leave it out — it is never penalised for absence.
  • Quantify every single bullet point. "Managed payroll for 90 employees including EPF/ESIC/PT compliance" — not just "managed payroll." "Built HR MIS dashboard tracking 6 KPIs for 3 departments" — not "created HR dashboard." Specificity is what passes Dubai ATS filters.
  • Explicitly mention UAE-relevant credentials. ACCA papers passed, MBA specialisation in Finance, UAE labour law knowledge, WPS familiarity, Power BI certification. Dubai employers scan for these keywords — if they aren't in the CV, it won't surface in searches.
  • Languages section. English fluency is non-negotiable in Dubai's professional environment. Mention: English (fluent), Hindi (native), Gujarati (native). Arabic is not expected for Finance analyst roles but even "Basic Arabic" is a positive signal.
  • No objective statement. Replace with a 3-line professional summary that includes: current role, years experience, target role, and 2–3 specific skills. Dubai recruiters do not read generic objectives.
